What Makes a Survey Respondent "Verified"?

A verified respondent is not simply someone who agreed to take a survey. Verification is a well-defined respondent verification system that verifies the respondent’s identity, demographics, previous involvement, and relevancy to the topic.

The following are some characteristics of a verified respondent:

  • Identification confirmation: the respondent must be a unique, human participant, not a bot or duplicate

  • Demographics Validation: age, geographical location, and occupation must meet certain criteria

  • Engagement Verification: participation by the respondent cannot be due to the allure of incentives

  • Topic Verification: the participant must have relevant experience in using the product or service being researched

Using survey sampling without such verification can be highly biased. For example, even if the consumer research panel seems to be large, analysis will show otherwise. How to Identify and Reach Your Ideal Research Audience

Knowing who you need is only half the challenge. The other half is accessing a recruitment pipeline that actually delivers those people.

Target audience screening filters respondents by criteria defining your research population, job title, household income, purchase behavior, or location. Effective screening reduces sample error before data collection begins.

Recruitment through online panels has advanced. Good B2B and B2C survey panels have multiple qualifiers, providing researchers with an opportunity to target specific markets while retaining their sample size.

As you develop your sampling plan, remember the following:

  • Make sure that you distinguish between necessary filters and those that are merely convenient.

  • Use Quota Sampling to balance demographics in any regionally or generationally based research projects.

  • Audit your panel source. Ask how respondents are recruited, duplicates are caught, and fraud is flagged.
